Customers under the age of majority

HTC does not wish to collect any data from anyone under the age of majority.  If you are under the age of majority (18 in most countries) you should press the Decline button at the bottom of the screen on which this Policy appears, indicating that you do NOT consent to HTC collecting and using the data being requested by HTC in this Policy. 

Why does HTC collect error reports?

HTC strives to improve hardware and software performance and reliability by collecting information that can be used to improve the products of HTC.  As part of that effort, the Tell HTC Service helps by creating an error report with information from your device when a program, application, or piece of hardware experiences an error.  You can choose whether or not to send this error report to HTC in the manner explained below.

What's in an error report?

When you experience an error, software on your device gathers information that describes the error, and other information (such as network and location information) that may help HTC identify and resolve the problem. The content of error reports vary depending upon the cause and the nature of the error.  Error reports may include the following types of information: 

Error information.  Information about the type of error and a description of the error.

Device Information. Includes information about the device, such as the device name, device ID, build ID, model, product, and version release.  

Log file.  Information about the state of your device at the moment the error occurred that may be helpful in determining the cause of the error.  Please note that the log file may contain personal information, but that any personal information which is unintentionally collected will not be used to identify, contact you, used for any other purpose, or passed on to any third parties.

You can view the details of the report before sending it, unless you have selected the automatic reporting feature.

How to send an error report 

The first time your device or an application crashes, fails to respond, or stops unexpectedly because of an error, you will be asked if you would like to send an error report.  You will have the option to choose whether you want to be asked each time a report is created or if you would like to automatically send reports without prompting.  If you select the automatic report option, future reports will be sent without notifying you or asking for your consent.  If you want to turn off the automatic reporting feature, you can change the Tell HTC setting on your device to ask each time before sending a report.  

The first time an error report is created, you will also be prompted to select your network preference, which controls how the error report will be sent.  You can choose to send the report when there is a data connection or when a Wi-Fi or cable connection is available.  If you indicate that you would like to send the report via the data connection, data charges may apply.    If you select the automatic reporting feature, the network preference you choose at the time you select automatic reporting will remain unless you change it by changing the Tell HTC setting on your device to ask each time before sending a report.  [Go to Menu->Settings->About Phone->Tell HTC.]

If you are not in your home country (so your phone is “roaming”) an error report will only be sent if there is an active Wi-Fi or cable connection only, but not if there is a data connection. 

How HTC uses error reports

HTC collects the error reports that customers send, analyzes them, and uses the aggregate information to determine what is causing problems for our customers. The error-reporting information enables HTC and its suppliers to determine which errors are the most serious and work to resolve those problems first. If you believe your problem is caused by your wireless network or an application or feature that is provided by a third party, you should still send the error report to HTC, so that HTC can forward the information to the third party provider.  HTC only provides error reporting information to third parties to help them fix errors and improve their products.  HTC will only give third parties aggregate or anonymised information about errors and problems and such third parties shall agree to keep any received data confidential before receiving such data.  HTC will not provide any personal information that may have been collected from a report. 

HTC may also disclose information collected from the Service as part of a corporate business transaction, such as a merger or acquisition, joint venture, corporate reorganization, financing, or sale of company assets (including for due diligence purposes for a proposed commercial business transaction).  HTC may also disclose information in the event of insolvency, bankruptcy, or receivership in which information could be transferred to third parties as one of the business assets in the transaction.

Information storage, processing, and release.

If an error report does contain personal information it may be used, stored, processed, transmitted and/or transferred in, to or between, the United States, Taiwan, the Netherlands, Singapore and any other country in which HTC or its service providers maintain facilities.  By using the Service, you consent to the use, storage, processing, transmission and/or transfer of information outside of the country where you reside.  While in another jurisdiction for processing, the information may be accessed by the courts, law enforcement and national security authorities.  When an error report is sent it is linked with a unique identification number, which identifies the error report as coming from a specific phone, but does not identify the actual phone.  All error reports collected will promptly be anonymised and disassociated from such identification number. 

The security of your data

HTC wants to keep your data safe and uses a variety of security technologies and administrative procedures to help protect the data from unauthorized access and use. For example, during the transmission of the data from your phone to HTC, HTC uses a secured channel (HTTPS) to protect it. This is a secure method for encrypted communication with an identified network (HTC in this case).  Once the data is received by HTC it is stored on a secured and reliable network, the servers are located in controlled facilities with limited access and access to the data is restricted to authorized HTC employees who need to use the data.
